# PDF 1 — Course Introduction

## What this course is, in one sentence

> "Teach modern networks/communications through hands-on activities."

Theory is followed by labs. The labs are the point, not the slides.

---

## Syllabus (the 4 big chunks)

1. **Introduction** — Internet architecture & service evolution
2. **Network virtualization & softwarization** — VLAN/VPN/VXLAN → SDN → SDR (with hands-on)
3. **Cloud computing** — VMs and containers (Docker hands-on)
4. **Vehicular networks** — V2X applications (with hands-on)

The unifying theme of the whole course is **softwarization**: take things that used to be hardware (switches, radios, full machines) and turn them into software.

---

## How you're graded

| Step | What | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Computer-based multiple-choice **quiz** | **1/3** |
| 2 | **Group project** (2–3 students, *highly recommended*) | **2/3** |
| 2 (alt) | Oral exam on entire program | (same weight) |

**Three rules:**
1. Project is graded as a group.
2. Final grade is registered only after the quiz is passed.
3. Orals can only be done after passing the quiz.

The project route is strongly preferred.

## Tools you'll use

- **Kathará** — network emulator (SDN/VPN labs)
- **Wireshark** — packet sniffer
- **Docker** — containers
- **Vagrant** — quick VM provisioning
- **GNU Radio** — SDR

## Key takeaways

- The course philosophy is **hands-on**.
- **Quiz first, then project** is the path of least resistance.
- Five tools to install: **Kathará, Wireshark, Docker, Vagrant, GNU Radio**.
- The unifying theme is **softwarization** — everything we'll study replaces specialized hardware with software/general-purpose hardware.
